# The Anatomy of Genius · 天才的解剖

A bilingual (EN + 中文) analytical companion reading three minds across five centuries — **Leonardo da Vinci**, **Richard Feynman**, and **Elon Musk** — for the shared mental machinery of genius.

Eight traits, each read across all three subjects: the curiosity engine, first-principles reasoning, the notebook habit, visual thinking, cross-domain synthesis, play & irreverence, obsessive immersion, and the human cost. Plus a flagship eight-axis **GeniusRadar** comparing all three, a five-century **timeline** (1452 → 2015) that makes the 400-year silence between Leonardo and Feynman visceral, a tabbed **NotebookViewer** (with Leonardo's lines in mirror script), an interactive **FirstPrinciplesLab**, a branching **CuriosityEngine**, a five-lens **analyst panel** (biographer · neuroscientist · historian · skeptic · educator), an eight-rung **cultivation ladder** of learnable habits, and a "meet the cast" portrait panel.

The site is **original analytical commentary**. It does not reproduce any source text. Iconic lines are paraphrased and attributed, not transcribed. The Musk material is drawn from Ashlee Vance's 2015 biography and stays inside what that book documents (it predates the Twitter acquisition).
